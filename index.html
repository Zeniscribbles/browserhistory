Introduction

TThe evolution of the internet has been a transformative journey, beginning in the late 1960s with ARPANET, a U.S. government project designed to create a network for sharing information between computers. Over time, the development of key technologies like TCP/IP protocols in the 1980s enabled more widespread connectivity. The introduction of the World Wide Web in 1991 by Tim Berners-Lee revolutionized how people accessed and shared information, making the internet accessible to the public. Since then, the internet has grown exponentially, shaping modern communication, commerce, education, and entertainment through innovations like broadband, wireless technology, and the rise of social media and mobile devices.

A Brief History of Internet Explorer

Internet Explorer (IE) was once the dominant browser for over a decade. Released by Microsoft in 1995, it was part of the Windows operating system and became widely used. However, with the rise of other browsers like Firefox and Chrome, IE began to lose its prominence.
